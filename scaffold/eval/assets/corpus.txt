The bench box came off a pallet of decommissioned 1U servers, two sockets of an
ISA generation that never learned the wide vector tricks the newer chips take for
granted. What it does have is memory channels, and at batch one that is the only
currency that matters: every generated token drags the active weights across the bus
exactly once, and the bus does not care how clever the kernel is once it is full.
The work, then, is not to chase peak FLOPs that the silicon will never reach, but to
move fewer bytes per token and to keep the few vector lanes that exist busy with the
integer dot products that quantized weights actually need.
